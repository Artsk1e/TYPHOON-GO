Typhoon Safety Tips

When a storm is on the horizon, proactive preparation is the single most effective way to protect your home and family. This guide outlines critical safety protocols to follow before, during, and after a typhoon to help you minimize risks and stay out of harm's way.

Before the Typhoon

  • Prepare a 3-day emergency kit (water, food, and medicine)
  • Secure loose objects around the house (e.g., roof tiles, pots)
  • Know your local evacuation center
  • Trim branches and clear gutters
  • Charge all electronic devices and power banks
  • Keep important documents in a waterproof bag

During the Typhoon

  • Stay indoors, move to a safe interior room away from windows
  • Keep a battery-powered radio for updates
  • Turn off the main power if water rises and enters your home
  • Do not go outside no matter how calm it seems
  • Keep children and pets in a designated safe area
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for safety instructions

After the Typhoon

  • Inspect your house for structural damage before entering
  • Beware of fallen power lines and floodwaters
  • Report damages to local authorities immediately
  • Use flashlights instead of candles to avoid fire hazards
  • Check on neighbors and community members
  • Dispose of spoiled food and contaminated water

First Aid & Survival Guide

In the wake of an emergency, knowing how to respond to injuries and manage limited resources can be life-saving. These essential survival strategies and first aid techniques are designed to provide you with the practical skills needed to handle medical situations and maintain safety until professional help arrives.

Essential First Aid Kit (Waterproof)

  • Wound Care: Antiseptic wipes, alcohol, cotton, gauze, bandages, adhesive plasters.
  • Medicine: Pain relievers (paracetamol), anti-diarrheal medication, antibiotics, and personal prescription medications.
  • Hygiene/Safety: Face masks, hand sanitizer, soap, mosquito repellent, and a whistle.
  • Tools: Small flashlight (or headlamp), extra batteries, scissors, tweezers.

Immediate First Aid Actions

  • Stop Bleeding: Apply direct pressure to wounds using a sterile cloth or bandage.
  • Treat Minor Cuts: Clean with antiseptic wipes or alcohol to prevent infection from dirty water.
  • Manage Fractures: Keep the injured area still and elevated, and seek medical attention as soon as possible.
  • Prevent Illness: Wash hands frequently with sanitizer and consume only clean water to avoid diarrhea and skin infections.

CPR & Choking Response

  • CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation): For unresponsive person with no pulse, perform chest compressions at 100-120 beats per minute.
  • Choking Victim: Perform the Heimlich maneuver by placing a fist above the navel and delivering upward thrusts.
  • Recovery Position: Place unconscious breathing victim on their side to maintain airway patency.
